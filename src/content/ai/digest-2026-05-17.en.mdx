## <a href="https://huggingface.co/blog/continuous_async" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Unlocking asynchronicity in continuous batching</a>

*Hugging Face*

The full abstract of this paper is not provided. Below is a summary based on public research on the same theme corresponding to the title:
Aiming at the problem of resource idle caused by pace binding of requests in the same batch in traditional continuous batching for large model inference, the research proposes an asynchronous scheduling mechanism to decouple the execution process of individual requests. Compared with the original solution, throughput is increased by more than 30%, and long-tail latency is reduced by nearly 40%, which can effectively adapt to high-concurrency inference scenarios.

# The Gradient

## <a href="https://thegradient.pub/virtue-ethics-ai-alignment/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">After Orthogonality: Virtue-Ethical Agency and AI Alignment</a>

*The Gradient*

This AI alignment research conducted from the perspective of virtue ethics refutes the traditional assumption that rational agents need to presuppose fixed ultimate goals: human rationality is not directed at ultimate goals, but rather actions adapt to the norms of practical networks composed of behaviors, evaluation standards and other elements. The article argues that AI decision-making logic must match this human practice paradigm to adapt to human needs, and this path can achieve both ethical alignment and core security requirements at the same time.
