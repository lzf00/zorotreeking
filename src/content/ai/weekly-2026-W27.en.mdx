The updates for the two content lines this week ran more smoothly than I expected. For the daily curated AI papers, except for a temporary small issue with the data source on the 5th that only allowed 13 pieces to be published, we stably maintained a volume of 21 pieces per day for the rest of the week, covering areas from LLM context window optimization to new solutions for AI implementation on edge devices. Friends working in tech R&D can go back to the mid-week content pieces from this week, and you will dig up a lot of cutting-edge ideas that have not yet circulated widely in the industry.

On the investing side, in addition to the daily news roundups, I specially added limit up/limit down structure data to the newly launched daily stock reports, instead of only including dry index numbers. On the 2nd, indexes fell across the board but the number of limit-up stocks was still close to 160, an abnormal data point that several long-time readers said helped them avoid the pitfall of emotional panic selling.

For friends who followed along with the content this week, you either got a sense of the current niche trending directions in AI research, or avoided short-term emotional interference in the A-share market to make more rational decisions, which makes the two hours I spend filtering content every day well worth it.
