# The Gradient

## <a href="https://thegradient.pub/virtue-ethics-ai-alignment/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">After Orthogonality: Virtue-Ethical Agency and AI Alignment</a>

*The Gradient*

This paper conducts AI alignment research from the perspective of virtue ethics, challenging the traditional perception that "rational agents need to be anchored to fixed goals". It proposes that human rational behavior is not directed at ultimate goals, but adapts to the practice network composed of actions, evaluation standards, resources, etc. To realize AI alignment with human values, meet security requirements, and collaborate smoothly with humans, the AI decision-making logic needs to match this practice-based action logic of humans.

# Lil'Log

## <a href="https://lilianweng.github.io/posts/2026-06-24-scaling-laws/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Scaling Laws, Carefully</a>

*Lilian Weng*

This paper sorts out the key empirical finding in the deep learning field, the scaling law: training loss decreases in accordance with the power law as model scale, dataset scale, and computing power investment increase, showing a linear relationship on a double-logarithmic coordinate. The study defines it as an analytical framework that depicts the correlation between computing power, loss, model, and data, with its core value being guiding the optimal allocation of limited computing power between model expansion and data scale increase.
