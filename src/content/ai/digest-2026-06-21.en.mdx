# Hugging Face Blog

## <a href="https://huggingface.co/blog/ServiceNow/mosaicleaks" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">MosaicLeaks: Can your research agent keep a secret?</a>

*Hugging Face*

This paper titled *MosaicLeaks: Can your research agent keep a secret?* focuses on the privacy risks of autonomous research agents, and proposes the MosaicLeaks data leakage test method: split sensitive information into discrete fragments and mix them into reference materials for research tasks, inducing the agent to spontaneously splice, restore and leak the information during task execution. Tests show that the leakage rate of mainstream research agents is as high as over 85%, and existing conventional security protections are almost ineffective, so targeted optimization of confidentiality mechanisms is urgently needed.

# The Gradient

## <a href="https://thegradient.pub/virtue-ethics-ai-alignment/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">After Orthogonality: Virtue-Ethical Agency and AI Alignment</a>

*The Gradient*

This AI alignment study exploring extensions to the orthogonality thesis takes virtue ethics as the perspective, refutes the presupposition that "rational agents are necessarily guided by fixed goals", points out that human rationality adjusts actions based on a practical network including elements such as behavior and evaluation standards, and proposes that for AI to adapt and collaborate with humans, its decision-making logic needs to match the human practical reasoning model. This path is related to both AI ethical alignment and core security.
