## <a href="https://huggingface.co/blog/Dharma-AI/specialization-beats-scale" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Specialization Beats Scale: A Strategic Variable Most AI Procurement Decisions Overlook</a>

*Hugging Face*

This paper focuses on the common blind spot in AI procurement decisions, pointing out that the current industry mostly prioritizes large-parameter general models, but ignores the core variable of scenario specialization. The research verifies that vertical small models customized for specific businesses are better than large general models called in the same scenario in terms of accuracy, deployment cost, data security and response efficiency. It is recommended that procurement take scenario adaptability as a core evaluation indicator, and there is no need to blindly pursue model scale.

# The Gradient

## <a href="https://thegradient.pub/virtue-ethics-ai-alignment/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">After Orthogonality: Virtue-Ethical Agency and AI Alignment</a>

*The Gradient*

This paper conducts AI alignment research from the perspective of virtue ethics, challenging the underlying presupposition of the orthogonality hypothesis that "rational agents are necessarily bound to fixed goals", pointing out that human rationality is not directed to the ultimate goal, but adjusts behavior in the practice network composed of actions, tendencies, evaluation criteria, etc. The study proposes that if AI is to adapt to collaborating with humans, its decision-making logic needs to be isomorphic with human practice-driven logic, which is not only an ethical alignment requirement, but also related to the core security of AI.
